c r droit @ by the King @ a proclamation declare the king majesty royal pleasure touch the inhabitant of Algiers Tunis sally and Tituan in the part of Africa whereas diverse complaint have of late come unto we from the inhabitant of Algiers Tunis sally and tituan and the country under the same government of diverse great wrong do unto they at sea by some of our subject which if it have be do be contrary to our royal intention and without any warrant from we we take this into our princely consideration and tender as well the good usage of all those with who our subject have or shall have commerce and intercourse as the good and safety of our subject trade into those part by the advice of our privy counsel do hereby publish and declare our royal pleasure and do strait charge and command all our love subject of what sort quality or degree soever they be who it may any way concern that they do from henceforth forbear all act of hostility or to offer any violence or ill usage unto any the person ship or good belong or which shall belong to Algiers Tunis Tituan sally or any other port or part of those country which be under the same government as they will answer the contrary at their peril and will avoid our high and heavy displeasure and such pain and punishment as by our law may be impose or inflict upon they for their contempt or neglect herein give at our court at white-hall the two and twenty day of October in the four year of our reign of great Britain France and Ireland God save the King
