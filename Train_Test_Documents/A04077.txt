LAW and order of war establish for the good conduct of the service in Jreland robert earl of Essex and ewe earl Martial of England viscount Hereforde and Bourgcher lord Ferrer of Chartley l. Bourgcher and Lovayn and her majesty lieutenant and governor general of the kingdom of Ireland to all officer of the army and all colonel captain officer and soldier of company and all her majesty subject and other who these law and order ensue respective and several shall concern forasmuch as no good service can be perform or war well manage where military discipline be not observe and military discipline can be keep where the rule or chief part thereof be not certain set down and general know I have with the advice of the counsel of war set down these law and order follow and do now publish they under my hand that all person in this army or kingdom within my charge may take knowledge of the say law and the penalty set down for the breaker of they first since we owe our high duty and great allegiance to he from who all power and authority flow as from the first fountain and must receive our success of he that be lord of host I do straight charge and command all commander and officer of the army to see that almighty GOD be due serve that sermon morning and evening prayer be diligent frequent and that those that do often and wilful absent themselves be due punish 2 let no man speak impious and malicious against the holy and bless trinity or any of the three person that be to say God the father God the son and God the holy ghost or against the know article of the christian faith upon pain of death 3 let no man blaspheme god holy name or use unlawful oath or execration or commit any irreligious act to the derogation of God honour upon pain of loss of his pay imprisonment and such further punishment as a martial court shall think his offence deserve 4 no man shall use any traitorous word against her majesty person or royal authority upon pain of death 5 no man whether he be soldier or other english or irish shall have conference or intelligence with any enemy or rebel that be in open action against her majesty or harbour or receive any such within the camp or any town fort castle or garrison or shall send or procure to be send any victual munition or other relief to any enemy or rebel in action upon pain of death except such as shall be avow and warrant by i or those that shall command the army in my absence to speak confer or have intelligence or commerce with the enemy or rebel 6 no man shall run to the enemy or rebel that be in action or depart the army without licence upon pain of death 7 no man shall commit any murder or kill any person or draw blood of any or draw any sword in private quarrel with intent to offer violence within the camp or garrison upon pain of death 8 no man shall ravish or force any woman upon pain of death and adultery or fornication shall be punish by imprisonment banishment from the army or such other penalty as by the marshal court shall be think meet 9 no man shall steal or take by force any treasure victual or munition of her majesty or take by force or steal from any person any money arm apparel or other good be above the value of 12. penny upon pain of death 10 no man shall deliver up any town castle fort or sconce without warrant or depart from any straight or passage which he be command to make good or take passport of the enemy or any rebel in action for any troop or make any ignominious composition with the enemy or rebel in action upon pain of death 11 no man shall throw away his arm or abandon his ensign Cornet or Gwidon or fly away in any battle or skirmish upon pain of death 12 no man be set sentinel shall sleep or appoint by the officer of the guard or any superior officer that have authority to place he shall depart and forsake that place without warrant upon pain of death 13 no man shall offer any violence or contemptuous disobey his commander or do any act or speak any word which be like to breed any mutiny in the army or impeach the obey of the general or principal officer direction upon pain of death 14 no man shall outrage or do violence to any that come to bring victual to the army upon pain of death 15 no man that carry arm or pretend to be a soldier shall remain three day in the army after it be on foot except he be enrol in some company upon pain of death 16 no soldier of the army shall do violence to the person or steal or violent take or wilful spoil the good of any irish good subject upon pain of death 17 no soldier shall burn any house or lodging or burn or wilful spoil any corn ship or boat or carriage or other thing that may serve for the provision of the army without he be command so to do by the general or some principal officer of the army upon pain of death 18 no man shall depart a mile out of the army or camp except he be command or lawful warrant so to do upon pain of death 19 no soldier shall break his order to follow any rout or chase or to seek any prey or spoil except he be command by such as have authority nor further than he be command upon pain of death 20 no man that have passport for any limit time shall exceed the time limit for his absence except he can prove he be stay by the hand of God upon pain of death 21 no provider keeper or officer of her majesty victual or munition shall embesell any part thereof or give any false account to the general with a purpose to deceive her majesty or to hinder the service upon pain of death 22 no man shall wilful or through gross negligence fail of come to the rendezvous assign to he by the general or other principal officer that shall command the army upon pain of death 23 no soldier in muster shall answer for a other or take two pay or muster in a false name to defraud her majesty upon pain of death 24 no captain shall through corruption wilfulness or gross negligence suffer his company to grow weak or practice to defraud her majesty in his muster upon pain of imprisonment loss of his place and ignominious banishment from the army 25 no captain of the army or officer shall give passport to any or dismiss any soldier once enrol without warrant from the general or other officer who he shall authorize upon pain of loss of his place and imprisonment 26 no soldier shall depart from his captain or servant from his master without his licence though he serve still in the army upon pain of a month imprisonment and loss of a month wage 27 no soldier shall play away give away pawn or sell away his arm without he be dismiss and have leave of his captain so to do upon pain of imprisonment 28 all captain shall be diligent in train their company and shall be careful in govern they well and in provide for they and also shall see in all service that they do the duty of soldier as they will hope for favour and advancement or escape ignominious displace from their charge 29 all captain or other officer that for the time shall have command of company shall see their company do orderly quarter as they be appoint and to entrench or fortify as much of the quarter as they be appoint and as they be command upon pain of loss of their place 30 no man shall spoil or take the good of any man that die or be kill in service upon pain of restore the double value but the good of such as die in the army if they make any will by write or word shall be dispose according to the will if they make no will it shall be distribute to the hurt sick and poor of the company whereof the soldier be or shall go to the hospital of the army 31 no man shall enter or go out of the army but by ordinary way upon pain of imprisonment 32 no man shall give any disgraceful word or commit any act to the disgrace of any person in this army or any part thereof upon pain of imprisonment public disarm and banishment from the army as man for ever disable to carry arm and as I forbid all man under my rule to renew any old quarrel or to begin any new so I do acquit and discharge all man that shall have quarrel offer to they or challenge make to they of any disgrace or opinion of any disadvantage since they do but do the duty of soldier which ought to subject themselves to martial discipline and they that provoke they shall be proceed withal as breaker of all good discipline and enemy to the good success of the service 33 and because drunkenness be the mother of many other vice and disorder in army every soldier or officer that shall be find drink shall be commit to prison for the first offence and if he fall into it the second time be a private soldier he shall beside his imprisonment forfeit two month pay if he be a officer he shall lose his place the three time shall have such far great punishment as a marshal court shall set down 34 every private man and soldier upon pain of imprisonment shall keep silence when the army be to take lodging or when it be march or embattle so as the officer may be hear and the commandment execute 35 no soldier serve on foot shall carry any boy nor no woman shall be suffer to follow the army 36 all captain officer and soldier shall do their endeavour to detect apprehend and bring to punishment all offender and shall assist the officer of the army for that purpose as they will answer their slackness and be censure at a marshal Court 37 all other fault disorder and offence that be not mention in these article shall be punish according to the general custom and law of war and therefore I command all man to look to their charge and he that have no charge to look to his own carriage and to keep himself within band of duty for the discipline of war shall be strict keep and the law thereof severe execute
