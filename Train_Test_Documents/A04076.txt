law and order of war establish for the good conduct of the service of Ireland henry falkland Henry lord vice-count of falkland one of the lord of his majesty most honourable privy council of England and lord deputy general of the realm of Ireland to all officer of the army and all colonel captain officer and soldier of company and all his majesty subject and other who these law and order ensue respective and several shall concern forasmuch as no good service can be perform or war well manage where military discipline be not observe and military discipline can be keep where the rule or chief part thereof be not certain set down and general know I have set down these law and order follow and do now publish they under my hand that all person in this army or kingdom within my charge may take knowledge of the say law and penalty set down for the breaker of they 1 first we owe our high duty and great allegiance to he from who all power and authority flow as from the first fountain and must receive our success of he that be the lord of host I do straight charge and command all commander and officer of the army to see that almighty God be serve that sermon morning and evening prayer be diligent frequent and that those that often and wilful absent themselves be due punish 2 let no man speak impious and malicious against the holy and bless trinity or any of the three person that be to say God the father God the son and God the holy ghost or against the know article of the christian faith upon pain of death 3 let no man blaspheme god holy name or use unlawful oath or erecration or commit any irreligious art to the derogation of God honour upon pain of loss of his pay imprisonment and such further punishment as a martial court shall think his offence deserve 4 no man shall use any traitorous word against his majesty person or royal authority upon pain of death 5 no man whether he be soldier or other english or irish shall have conference or intelligence with any enemy or rebel that be in open action against his majesty or harbour or receive any such within the camp or in any town forte castle or garrison or shall send or procure to be send any victual munition or other relief to any enemy or rebel in action upon pain of death except such as shall be avow and warrant by i or those that shall command the army in my absence to speak confer or have intelligence or converse with the enemy or rebel 6 no man shall run to the enemy or rebel that be in action or depart the army without licence or from the garrison or colour upon pain of death 7 no man shall commit any murder or kill any person or draw blood of any or draw any sword in private quarrel with intent to offer violence within the camp or garrison upon pain of death 8 no man shall ravish or force any woman upon pain of death and adulterer and fornicator shall be punish by imprisonment banishment from the army or such other penalty as up the martial court shall be think meet 9 no man shall steal or take by force any treasure victual or munition of his majesty or take by force or steal from any person any money arm apparel or other good be above the value of twelve penny in march camp or garrison upon pain of death 10 no man shall deliver any town castle forte or sconce without warrant or depart from any straight or passage which he be command to make good or take pass port of the enemy or any rebel in action from any troop or make any ignominious composition with the enemy or rebel in action upon pain of death 11 no man shall throw away his arm or abandon his ensign Cornet or guidon or fly away in any battle or Skirmish upon pain of death 12 no man be set sentinel shall sleep or appoint by the officer of the guard or any superior officer that have authority to place he shall depart and forsake that place without warrant upon pain of death 13 no man shall offer any violence or contemptuous disobey his commander or do any art or speak any word which be like to breed any mutiny in the army or garrison or impeach the obare of the general or principal officer direction upon pain of death 14 no man shall outrage or do violence to any that come and to bring victual to the army or garrison upon pain of death 15 no man that carry arm or pretend to be a soldier shall remain three day in the army after it be on foot except he be enrol in some company upon pain of death 16 no soldier shall burn any house or lodging or burn or wilful spoil any corn shipe or boate or carriage or any other thing that may serve for the provision of the army or his garrison without he be command so to do by the general or some principal officer of the army upon pain of death 17 no man shall depart a mile out of the army or camp or garrison except he be command and lawful warrant so to do upon pain of death 18 no soldier shall break his order to follow any rout or chase or to seek any prey or spoil except he be command by such as have authority no further than he be command upon pain of death 19 no man that have passport for any limit time shall exceed the time limit for his absence except he can prove he be stay by the hand of God upon pain of death 20 no Providor keeper or officer of his majesty victual or munition shall embrazill any part thereof or give any false account to the general with a purpose to deceive his majesty or to hinder the service upon pain of death 21 no man shall wilful or through gross ignorance f a^le in come to the randezvous or garrison assign to he by the general or other principal officer that shall command the army upon pain of death 22 no Soldier in master shall answer for another or take two or muster in a false name to defraud his majesty upon pain of death 23 no captain shall through corruption wilful or gross negligence suffer his company to grow weak or practice to defraud his majesty in his muster upon pain of imprisonment loss of his place and ignominious banishment from the army 24 no captain of the army or officer shall give passport to any or dismiss any soldier once enrol without warrant from the the general or other officer who he shall authorise upon pain of loss of his place and imprisonment 25 no soldier shall depart from his captain or servant from his master without licence though he serve still in the army upon pain of death 26 item that no soldier do sell or lay to pawn his horse or hackney or any part of his furniture arm or apparel for any respect or pretence of want whatsoever upon pain of death 27 nor that any inhabitant in town or country shall presume to buy or to take to pawn any part of the same upon pain both of imprisonment at the discretion of the lord deputy and chief commander and forfeit of double the value of any such thing so buy or lay to pawn 28 item that all band of footman shall in pass through the pale or other country in obedience to go to any garrison or place of service march ten mile a day or thereabouts hold the direct way to the same garrison or place of service and that no captain officer or soldier do upon any pretence of want commit any waist spoil or violence or do extort any victual or money or pawn in lieu of victual from any good subject but shall content themselves with meat and drink competent not take any allowance for dead pay but for the most number of man they have in their company for a supper and a breakfast in one place only upon pain of such sharp punishment as the lord deputy or other magistrate thereunto authorise agreeable to the nature and quality of the same offence shall think good to inflict be it by death imprisonment or other corporal punishment but if upon any occasion of march through the country the soldier shall be in want that then the officer with the advice consent and assistance of the sheriff subshiriffe collector or other his majesty officer in that barony or the next adjoin if there be any or may be find shall seek and provide such diet and lodging for they as shall be think fit at reasonable rate heretofore accustom for the which the captain or officer give ready money or for want thereof deliver his ticket to be satisfy upon the captain or soldier entertainment so as all occasion of offence and grief shall be far remove from all good subject 29 item if any horseman shall loose his horse or hackney or footman any part of his armour by negligence or other lewdness whereby he shall be unable to discharge the duty of his place that until he shall recover the same or otherwise furnish himself with as good he shall reserve in the state and condition of a Proner or sustain further punishment at the direction of the lord deputy or other officer thereunto authorise 30 item whensoever any prey or spoil shall be take from the revel either when the army be in the field or by any reside in garrison upon the border that no officer or soldier do attempt to imbeazill or purloin any part thereof upon pain of death but shall endeavour themselves to the uttermost of their power to keep the same together to be dispose at the direction of i the lord deputy or any other by i thereunto authorise 31 item that no captain officer or soldier or any other that shall take any prisoner shall presume to deliver upon ransom or conceal he but that within four and twenty hour he shall make the same know to the lord deputy or chief commander and deliver the same prisoner under the charge of the provost Martial upon pain of death 32 no inhabitant in city town or country shall presume to receive any soldier into their service not have a sufficient authority and passport neither shall any conceal or hide any such run away or use mean to convey they out of the kingdom or to any other secret place but shall apprehend all such and deliver they over to the provost-marshall upon pain of imprisonment and such other punishment as shall be think fit by the lord deputy or other chief officer hereunto authorise to be inflict 33 no magistrate or officer of any city or corporate town shall presume to imprison or detain the body of any soldier above four and twenty hour or to inflict any punishment upon he for any offence but shall deliver all such to the provost-marshall to be try by the speedy course of martial law 34 all captain shall be diligent in train their company and shall be careful in govern they well and in provide for they and also shall see in all service that they do the duty of soldier as they will hope for favour and advancement or escape ignominious displace from their charge 35 all captain and other officer that for the time shall have command of company shall see their company do orderly quarter as they be appoint and to entrench and fortify asmuch of the quarter as they be appoint and as they be command upon pain of loss of their place 36 no man shall spoil or take the good of any that die or be kill in service upon pain of restore double the value but the good of such as die in the army or garrison if they make any will by writing or word shall be dispose accord to the will if they make no will it shall be distribute to the hurt sick and poor of of the company whereof the soldier be or shall go to the hospital of the army 37 no man shall enter or go out of the army but by ordinary way upon pain of imprisonment 38 no man shall give any disgraceful word or commit any act to the disgrace of any person in his army or garrison or any part thereof upon pain of imprisonment public disarm and banishment from the army as man for ever disable to carry arm and as I forbid all man under my rule to renew any old quarrel or to begin any new so I do acquit and discharge all man that shall have quarrel offer to they or challenge make to they of any disgrace or opinion of any disadvantage since they do but the duty of soldier which ought to subject themselves to marshal discipline and they that provoke they shall be proceed withal as breaker of all good discipline and enemy to the good success of the service 39 and because drunkenness be the mother of many other vice and disorder in army every soldier or officer that shall be find drink shall be commit to prison for the first offence and if he fall into it the second time be a private soldier he shall beside his imprisonment forfeit two month pay if he be a officer he shall loose his place the three time shall have such far great punishment as the martial court shall set down 40 every private man and soldier upon pain of imprisonment shall keep silence when the army be to take lodging or when it be march or embatle so as the officer may be hear and their commadement execute 41 no soldier serve on foot shall carry any boy nor any woman shall be suffer to follow the army upon pain of such punishment as shall be inflict by the lord deputy or other officer 42 all captain officer and soldier shall do their endeavour to detect apprehend and bring to punishment all offender and shall assist the officer of the army for that purpose as they will answer their slackness and be censure at the marshall-court 43 all other fault disorder and offence that be not mention in these article shall be punish accord to the general custom and law of war and therefore I command all man to look to their charge and he that have no charge to look to his own carriage and to keep himself within bound of duty for the discipline of war shall be strict keep and the law thereof severe execute 44 no victualler shall presume to issue or sell unto the soldier or to the company unsound unsavoury or unwholesome victual upon pain of imprisonment and such other punishment as by a marshall-court shall be think fit to be impose upon he or they that shall so offend 45 no captain lieutenant or other officer of the army shall in the tender and presentment of their muster use any fraud practice or deceit whereby the muster-master his commissary or deputy may be mislead mistake or prevent of the due understanding of the true estate of of that company upon pain of the loss of his place and such other ignominious punishment as by a marshall-court shall be think meet 46 no muster-master shall either for favour friendship love or hatred or any other by respect whatsoever impose a less or great check upon any captain or officer than his default shall just merit nor get any check at all where no defect or fault shall appear upon a due view take of the company and that no check shall be impose by discretion without view upon pain that every offender that shall be find to transgress in all or any part of this article shall forfeit the place which he hold and be further subject to such corporal or pecuniary punishment as by the discretion of a marshall-court shall be think convenient we require the marshal of the army the judge-marshall the captain of horse and foot in his majesty pay and all other his majesty officer in this kingdom who it may concern to observe these law and to see they put in due execution from time to time publish the 11. day of june
