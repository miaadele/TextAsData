JAMES by the grace of God king of England Scotland France and Ireland defender of the faith etc. to all to who these present shall come greeting whereas we have be inform aswell by a lamentable petition on the behalf of fifteen hundred of our love subject english man remain in miserable servitude and subjection in Argier Tunis sally Tituane exhibit to the lord spiritual and temporal in our last parliament-assemble thereby pitiful crave some good mean to be take for their relief and redemption as also by a order take and set down the seven and twenty day of May 1624. by the say lord then in parliament assemble thereby order that letter patent shall be make for their relief to the effect in these present follow the humble and lamentable complaint of above fifteen hundred poor captive soul now under the miserable oppression of the turk in Argier Tunis sally and tituane who lamentable show that be surprise in one hundred and fifty of his majesty subject ship by turkish pirate and now remain in the say port and hold in miserable slavery and sell from party to party and keep in chain of iron their food bread and water to their extreme grief some these three year some more some less not only to the great damage of themselves but of their poor wife and child who be all ready to starve and perish for want of mean for their sustentation and they further most lamentable complain that most of the youthfull sort be of late force and compel by intolerable and insufferable punishment and torment to deny their saviour and turn to their mahometan religion and to deny their own king and country which God know be to their great grief and utter discomfort for ever unless their relief and freedom may be purchase and obtain by the favourable and pious consideration and assistance of this honourable assembly without which they be like to be all utter ruin both in body and soul and their poor wife and child likely through extremity and want miserable to perish now the lamentable complaint and cry of so many poor soul and the grievous torment they now sustain for their conscience sake be well weigh and consider of by this great and grave assembly they humble upon their bare knee for Christ jesus sake most humble desire that out of the bowel of compassion your honour will be please to take some speedy course and mean for the free of they out of the merciless hand of those cruel infidel all relief and comfort heretofore send they by their poor wife and friend be detain and keep from they by reason whereof they be enforce to grind corn in bestial manner like horse for to get some food to preserve their wretched life with infinite misery not here mention for brevity sake and the great and extreme necessity of their poor wife and child and they with all their wife and child be many hundred in number shall daily pray for the happy success of your honour in all your enterprise upon the read of which petition it be this seven and twenty day of May 1624. order by the lord spiritual and temporal in this present parliament assemble that letter patent be grant for a collection throughout the whole kingdom of England and dominion of Wales for redemption of those miserable captive the say letter patent to be grant upon commendation from the lord of the parliament upon petition prefer to their lordship the say letter patent with print brief to be send to the lord bishop in their several diocese within each province and their lordship to send the brief to all the minister of every parish within their diocese which minister by especial exhortation be to stir up the charity of their parishioner for a liberal contribution towards this so charitable a work the money in every parish to be collect by the churchwarden and overseer of the poor who present after the collection shall declare public in the church what be collect and the same sum to be put on the backside of the brief with their and the minister hand to it and then the church warden and overseer of the poor or one of they to deliver the same collection to some such minister in every deanery as shall be nominate by the lord bishop for the receipt of the same and those minister to deliver the same within ten day after the receipt thereof to the lord bishop of the diocese and every bishop to deliver ever the collection make in his diocese to his metropolitan and the metropolitan of York to transmit what be collect within his province to the archbishop of Canterbury unto who by order of the house all the money to be collect for this use be to be deliver who with the advice of six lord of parliament and the lord of his majesty privy council be to dispose of the say money so collect for relief and redemption of the captive as be specify in the petition and not otherwise especial commendation to all major etc. and this to continue for one whole year and by way of example the lord of parliament be now ready to lead the way by ^^^^ing in their several rank and so do order forty shilling of every member above the degree of a baron and those of the degree of baron lord spiritual and temporal twenty shilling apiece to be present pay into the hand of the lord archbishop of Canterbury to be dispose of as the rest of the money to be collect for the use above mention and it be further order that absent lord may not be exempt from this contribution but that the lord that have their proxy deposit the same for they and it be also order that all be do without any charge or reward to any person for the same ex. h. elsing cler. and whereas also the say lord have perfect understand and due consider the woeful and pitiful estate of those poor man and in commiseration of they have free of themselves pay down present every of they above the estate of a baron forty shilling a piece and every other of the estate of a baron be spiritual or temporal lord twenty shilling to be pay into the hand of the most reverend father in God the lord archbishop of Canterbury towards this so charitable a action as by the say order more at large appear know you therefore that we also above all other most grieve at the pitiful and woeful estate of the say poor man our subject and much commiserate the same and much rejoice in the forwardness of our say lord towards the performance of so Christian and charitable a action who good example we earnest desire that all other our love subject of quality will follow in this behalf according to their degree of our princely grace do ratify and much approve their say order so set down and in confirmation and for the execution thereof do grant that according to the say order collection may be make of the charitable devotion and liberality of all our love subject throughout our realm of England and dominion of Wales in all place whatsoever towards the relief and redemption of our say poor subject the captive aforesaid to be execute in manner and form follow that be to say we will grant appoint and require that Walter Dobson gentleman apparitor to the lord archbishop of Canterbury his grace or his deputy or deputy name and appoint by he in write under his hand and seal shall and may procure to be print so many brief of these our letter patent as may suffice for the accomplish and performance of this good work and shall carry and show these our letter patent under our great seal to the say lord archbishop of Canterbury and shall also leave with his grace so many of those print brief as there be church of chapel wherein public divine service be usual say within the circuit or precinct of the several province of Canterbury and York and we require every of the say lord archbishop to receive the say brief and with all speed to distribute unto each bishop within his province and to send so many unto the archbishop of York as may serve to be disperse within the diocese of his province the say brief to be send by the say bishop to the minister or curate of every parish-church and chapel within their several diocese and we also will and require every minister or curate of any church or chapel to who such print brief shall be deliver that without any long delay upon some Sunday or holiday when the parishioner be full assemble to hear divine service they public declare in their several church or chapel unto the congregation there assemble the tenor of these brief with a especial exhortation to they to yield their liberal and extraordinary contribution to so charitable a work and we also will and require you the church warden and overseer of the poor to make diligent collection of the charitable devotion of the people in the say church or chapel so assemble and that after your collection so make you do public declare in the church the whole sum of money so by you collect and then procure the same sum to be set down upon the backside of the brief under your own hand and the hand of the minister of the say church or chapel and then you the say church warden or overseer or one of you after the receipt of the same money collect shall deliver the sum collect with the say brief unto such minister of the deanery where the say collection shall be make as the lord bishop of the say diocese shall assign or appoint to receive the same and the bishop of every diocese be hereby authorize to name and appoint a minister in every deanery to receive the say collection according and we also require you the say minister which shall be so appoint to receive the say money from the say overseer that you receive the same with the foresay brief and that within ten day after the receipt thereof you pay and deliver the same with the brief whereby the same have be collect unto the lord bishop of the diocese where such collection be make and we also require you the bishop of every diocese respective that you receive of the say minister the say money with the brief whereby the same have be collect and deliver over all the say collection make within your diocese with the foresay brief to the metropolitan of the province wherein the collection shall be make and we likewise require you the say archbishop of York that you without delay do transmit to the say lord archbishop of Canterbury all the sum of money collect within your province with the brief whereby the same have be collect with he to remain until the same be dispose of in such fort as by the foresay order of the seven and twenty of May 1624. the same be to be dispose of any statute law ordinance or provision heretofore make to the contrary in any wise notwithstanding in witness whereof we have cause these our letter to be make patent for the space of one whole year next after the date hereof to endure witness ourself at Westminster the nine and twenty day of june in the two and twenty year of our reign of England France and Ireland and of Scotland he seven and fifty steward God save the King
